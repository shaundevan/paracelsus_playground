How we treat behavioural addictions

Behavioural addictions, also known as process addictions, are a set of behaviours that a person becomes dependent on and craves. They are a ‘process’ addiction because it is a set of actions or a process that people become addicted to, rather than a substance.

The rush that the addictive behaviour provides to the brain acts in much the same way as substance-related addiction. When we engage in an activity that keeps us alive or helps us pass on our genes, our reward system sends out a chemical messenger called dopamine which gives us a hit of satisfaction to encourage us to repeat that action. These dopamine hits can become addictive given the right environmental, psychological and biological factors. Therefore, any activity that releases a rush of dopamine, such as winning big in a casino, taking risks or getting likes on our social media accounts, can also become addictive. 

Depending on the specific addiction, behavioural addictions can cause various issues that impact all areas of life, from finances and career to relationships and family. In all cases, process addiction will introduce a great deal of stress, fear and pain into the individual’s life, resulting in the worsening of their physical and psychological health.

The symptoms, as well as the underlying issues, potential harm and problems, resulting from behavioural addictions are often very similar to those of substance-related addictions. Symptoms of behavioural addiction include:

  • Spending an excessive amount of time engaging in the behaviour to the detriment of your relationships or career.
  • Becoming dependent on the activity as a way to cope with emotions and “feel normal”.
  • Struggling to stop the behaviour despite attempts to quit.
  • Minimising, lying and hiding the problem from loved ones.
  • Experiencing symptoms of withdrawal (i.e. depression, trouble sleeping, irritability, personality changes) when trying to stop.
  • A loss of control over how frequently you engage in the behaviour and how much risk you take.
  • Engaging in the activity even though you obtain no pleasure from it and the development of specific triggers.

If these signs sound familiar, we can help guide you toward recovery.

How we treat behavioural addictions

We can’t tell you exactly what your four-week stay will involve because each person’s journey with behavioural addictions is unique. Therefore, we curate a bespoke behavioural addiction treatment plan for everyone who comes to stay at Paracelsus Recovery; there is no pre-defined treatment path. Similarly, we do not adopt a specific treatment model. Instead, we advocate for a completely pragmatic approach to addiction; whatever works best for you to achieve your goals.

Each of your days with us is thoughtfully structured to include handpicked therapeutic elements designed to help you overcome your behavioural addiction. From withdrawal to intensive therapy, every step is tailored to you. These therapies complement each other, working together to help you regain control, restore your health, and rebuild your life step by step. See below for more details.

Our approach considers the whole person: mind, body and soul

We do not adopt a ‘one-size-fits-all’ approach. Instead, we adapt each programme to each client’s unique set of needs. While our approach prioritises cutting-edge modalities and holistic care, those unique needs ultimately guide our work together.

At Paracelsus Recovery, our 360-degree treatment approach is designed to help you address, process and overcome the root causes of your dependency. By carrying out rigorous testing, we are able to identify all underlying issues and pave a path towards overcoming them. We design an individualised treatment plan featuring a unique blend of cutting-edge therapies to rebalance your psychological, cellular and biological health. For behavioural addictions, these typically include biofeedback, bioresonance, nutritional therapy, Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy (CBT), sleep therapies, and art therapy.

In every case, our treatment extends far beyond simply addressing the root causes of your behavioural addiction. In parallel to treating any underlying factors that have contributed to your addiction, we focus on any effects, such as neurological to physical damage, that can result from dependencies. Such treatments typically involve intensive psychotherapy, biochemical restoration, and complementary therapies, all of which share the aim of reducing stress and supporting long-term recovery.
