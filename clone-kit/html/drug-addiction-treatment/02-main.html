How we treat drug addiction

Due to the complex effects drugs have on the brain and the complications, such as anxiety and depression, that can occur during withdrawal it is essential to seek professional support at a residential drug treatment centre to successfully overcome a drug addiction. Through our combination of medical, psychological, and personal care, we are proud to be the only residential drug treatment centre in the world that addresses co-occurring conditions and every possible root cause of your suffering.

We become addicted to a drug when we are no longer able to control our use. Instead, we feel compelled to consume the substance, for fear of the sadness, anger, and physical sickness symptoms we believe we will feel without it. Those symptoms can be very real, such as in the case of withdrawal from opioids or nicotine, or they can be psychological; such as the panic that can set in when we are unable to find a ‘bag’ or get to ‘that party’.  

There are an array of emotional, physical, and social root causes of drug dependency which span emotional, physical, and social factors. What underpins substance abuse is the search for dopamine hits that create certain patterns of thinking and feeling. When we are struggling with unprocessed traumas, self-esteem issues, undiagnosed mental health issues, anxieties, boredom, and on that list can go, it drains our brain of feel-good neurochemicals. We start to feel exhausted, unsatisfied, and craving ‘something’. Drugs then start to fill that ‘something’ hole.

If your substance abuse is negatively impacting your life and relationships, you could have an addiction and require treatment.

Symptoms of drug addiction include: 

  • Feeling a lack of control over your substance abuse. 
  • Ever-increase tolerance levels.
  • Intense cravings for the substance, often to the point where you will use the drug despite its consequences on your relationships, career, and life. 
  • Withdrawal symptoms such as irritability, sleep disturbances, muscle aches, nausea, and mood changes. 
  • A growing sense that you are losing connection with who you really are. 

Additional symptoms include:

What are the psychological symptoms of drug addiction?

  • Intense cravings for the substance, even after short periods of abstinence.
  • Irritability or mood swings, often accompanied by bursts of aggression.
  • Anxiety or paranoia, sometimes escalating into delusions.
  • Impaired judgement and difficulty making decisions.
  • Restlessness or agitation that disrupts your ability to relax.

What are the physical symptoms of drug addiction?

  • Increased heart rate and blood pressure, raising the risk of heart attack or stroke (depending on the substance).
  • Physical symptoms like nausea, sweating, or trembling during withdrawal.
  • Changes in appearance, such as weight loss, bloodshot eyes, or poor hygiene.
  • Fatigue or exhaustion following substance use.
  • Damage to specific areas of the body associated with the substance (e.g., nasal damage from snorting drugs).

What are the behavioural symptoms of drug addiction?

  • Frequent and unexplained absences from work, school, or social commitments.
  • Risky or impulsive behaviours, such as unprotected sex or driving under the influence.
  • Financial difficulties from spending large sums on substances.
  • Neglect of responsibilities, relationships, or personal well-being.
  • Using drugs in secret or lying about substance use to others.

If these signs sound familiar, we can help guide you toward recovery.

How we treat drug addiction

We can’t tell you exactly what your four-week stay will involve because every patient’s journey is unique. As part of your drug addiction rehab at Paracelsus Recovery, we believe in a personalised approach, and each client receives a bespoke treatment plan, with every day thoughtfully structured to incorporate a range of therapies designed specifically for you.

While our approach includes cutting-edge medical care and therapeutic modalities, we recognise that recovery is different for everyone. Therefore, our approach is pragmatic and we do not require total sobriety as the sole measure of success. Instead, we support you in making gradual, sustainable progress towards healthier coping mechanisms.

Our approach considers the whole person: mind, body and soul

We do not adopt a ‘one-size-fits-all’ approach to drug addiction. Instead, we tailor each programme to your unique needs. While our approach integrates cutting-edge modalities and holistic care, your personal challenges guide the path we take together.

Alongside a carefully managed withdrawal process, a core aspect of our treatment is intensive testing to uncover all underlying causes of your drug dependency. This information shapes a highly specific treatment programme designed to help you understand the root causes of your addiction and how to break free from it.

We don’t just address the causes of addiction but also the consequences, from neurological to physiological damage often caused by substance abuse. These are treated in parallel with the underlying factors driving your addiction. Our treatments typically involve intensive psychotherapy, biochemical restoration, and complementary therapies, all aimed at reducing stress and supporting your recovery.
